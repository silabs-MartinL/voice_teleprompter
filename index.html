About

This teleprompter uses speech recognition to automatically follow your script. It is designed to keep moving through technical scripts by treating some words as required and some words as optional.

Recognition Guide

White words are required. The recognizer normally expects to hear these words.
Cyan words are optional. They may be skipped if the following required word is recognized.
Yellow shows the current word or reading position.
Grey words have already been read.

Why are some words cyan?

Some cyan words are filler words such as a, an, the, of, to, in, with, and, so, and when. These are optional because they do not help much with tracking your position and are usually not recognized individually.

Other cyan words are technical words that may be difficult for speech recognition, such as acronyms, product names, identifiers, or CamelCase terms.

Making a word optional

If a white word is not being recognized reliably, edit the script so it matches one of these optional-word patterns:

ALL CAPITALSRSSI, BLE, GPIO
Letters and numbersEFR32, STM32, SiWx917
CamelCaseOpenThread, BlueTooth

Making a word required

If a cyan word should be required, edit it so it no longer matches the optional-word rules.

RSSIrssi
OpenThreadOpen Thread
BlueToothBluetooth

Tip: Only make a word optional if it consistently causes recognition problems. Leaving ordinary English words as required generally gives the most accurate tracking.
